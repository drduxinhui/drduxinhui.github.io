文献解读 RSL1D1 promotes the progression of colorectal cancer through RAN-mediated autophagy suppression

Take home message

  • Functional validation studies (3 main figures)

    • Clinical significance study related figures (Fig 1)

    • In vitro and in vivo cancer study designs (kd and o/e for Fig 2 and 3, respectively)

  • Mechanism studies (4 main figures)

    • Biologicial process involvement of the model (Fig 4)

      • Autophage, Ferroptosis …

    • Interacting protein detection and validation (molecular and functional) (Fig 5)

    • Interacting proteins involvement with the proposed biological process (Fig 6)

    • Regulation / Bi-direction reguatlion between the interacting proteins (Fig 7)

Results presentation

  1. RSL1D1 is highly expressed in tumor samples from CRC patients, and high expression of RSL1D1 is correlated with poor CRC prognosis (Showing the clinical importance of the target protein chosen)

  • WB and Qpcr of cell lines (3 or 4 cell lines？)

  • Bioinformtic results from public datsets

  • WB of paired tumor samples ( more than 4 pairs?)

  • IHC of paired tumor samples

  • Survival analysis

  1. Overexpression of RSL1D1 promotes CRC cell proliferation, invasion, and metastasis in vitro and in vivo

  • In vitro studies

    • #Use two cell lines with the least protein levels for overexpression study.

    • #Present the two sets of results side by side

    • Cell proliferation assay: CCK-8 assay and colony formation assay.

    • Invasion: Matrigel invaion assays

    • Migration: Wound healing assays

  • In vivo studies

    • #Use one cell line with different treatment conditions (n = 5 for each group)

    • In vivo tumor growth: subcutaneous xenograft mouse models

    • In vivo metastasis: metastasis mouse models

    • Data presentation of in vivo tumor growth study

      • tumor volume change by time (in weeks)

      • tumor weight by group

      • IHC score of Ki67 by group

      • gross photo of the tumor

      • HE and IHC (target protein and Ki67) pictures.

    • Data presentation of in vivo tumor metastasis study

      • Whole-body fluoresence optical imaging of the nude mouse with tumor by group

      • Macroscopic pictures of the target organ with tumor by group

      • Microscopic pitures of the organ shown by H&E staining with scale bar at the right bottom

      • Table showing the rate of tumor metastasis in different group

      • Tumor volume plot by groups

  1. Knockdown of RSL1D1 inhibits CRC cell proliferation, invasion, and metastasis in vitro and in vivo

  • #Choose the cell lines with higher target protein levels for knockdown studies

  • #Use two cell lines and present the data side by side

  1. RSL1D1 promotes CRC cell proliferation and invasion by suppressing autophagy
  • #Use two different cell lines and kd or o/e conditions
  1. The interaction between RSL1D1 and RAN is essential for autophagy and the proliferation and invasion of CRC cells

  • Protein protein interation studies (Finding the downstream proteins)

    • Co-IP combined with LC-MS/MS to get the candidate interacting protein

    • Co-IP assays with the potential interacting proteins

    • IF localization studies by confocal microscopy

    • Functional validation by knocking down the interacting protein to reverse the phenotype changes with target protein o/e

  1. The interaction between RSL1D1 and RAN helps to influence the accumulation of nuclear STAT3 and the STAT3-regulated autophagy program

  2. RSL1D1 inhibits the deacetylation of RAN by competitively binding with Sirt7 and then promotes CRC cell proliferation and invasion by suppressing autophagy
